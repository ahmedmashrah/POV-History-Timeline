1986-1992

A Test Bed for Storytelling & Dialogue Through Television

1988

POV Debuts on PBS

At the 1986 U.S. Film Festival (later The Sundance Film Festival), Marc Weiss meets FRONTLINE's David Fanning at a festival panel and is convinced there is a future for bold, independent, point-of-view documentary storytelling on television. Soon after, he is organizing meetings with the producers and public TV representatives who were at odds in the 1980s but who would collaborate and form the editorial core of a new PBS documentary series — POV.

1988

When POV broadcast its first season, the Internet was just emerging from the rarefied world of university research and defense communications. Cable was new... Faxes were a novelty. Email, like the CD-ROM, was far in the future. DVD was just a glimmer in some corporate eye.

Cara Mertes, Executive Director, 1999-2006

1988

You Saw Them On POV First

Fahrenheit 9/11 director Michael Moore (Roger & Me and Pets or Meat: The Return to Flint, 1992), The Fog of War: Eleven Lessons from the Life of Robert S. McNamara director Errol Morris (Gates of Heaven, 1988), Ghost World director Terry Zwigoff (Louie Bluie, 1988)

1989

The Birth of the Web

Between 1989 and 1991, Tim Berners-Lee invents Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP), Hypertext Markup Language (HTML) and the World Wide Web.

1993-1998

Experiments in Viewer Engagement

1993

User-Generated Video: "Talking Back"

In 1993, POV started inviting viewers to record video comments to be aired on television in a segment initially called "Talking Back: Video Letters to P.O.V." The videos — sometimes critical, sometimes touching, sometimes funny — became a hallmark of POV's early seasons and another means for new views to be heard on national television.
These clips have not been seen since the original September 12, 1993, broadcast.
